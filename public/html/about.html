About MicroHabits

Small, consistent actions that lead to remarkable transformations over time.

What Are MicroHabits?

MicroHabits are tiny, easy-to-perform behaviors that require minimal motivation or willpower to complete. They are the building blocks of significant life changes, designed to be so small that they're almost impossible to skip.

Unlike traditional goal-setting that often leads to frustration and burnout, the MicroHabits approach focuses on consistency over intensity. By starting with actions that take less than two minutes to complete, you build momentum and create neural pathways that make lasting change possible.

The Power of Small

MicroHabits work because they bypass the resistance that often comes with larger behavioral changes. When an action feels too small to fail, you're more likely to do it consistently.

This approach is based on the principle that frequency is more important than duration when establishing new patterns. Doing something for just one minute every day creates a stronger habit than doing it for an hour once a week.

MicroHabits in Practice

Health & Wellness

  • Drink one glass of water after waking up
  • Do two stretches while waiting for coffee
  • Take the stairs instead of elevator one floor
  • Eat one vegetable with lunch
  • Stand up for one minute every hour

Productivity

  • Write one sentence for your report
  • Clear one item from your desk
  • Spend one minute planning your day
  • Read one professional article
  • Reply to one email immediately

Personal Growth

  • Read one page of a book
  • Practice one vocabulary word
  • Write one sentence in a journal
  • Listen to one educational podcast
  • Meditate for one breath

How to Implement MicroHabits

1

Identify

Choose one small behavior that aligns with your larger goals.

2

Simplify

Make it so easy you can't say no. Less than 2 minutes is ideal.

3

Anchor

Attach it to an existing habit or daily trigger.

4

Celebrate

Acknowledge each completion to reinforce the behavior.

5

Expand

Gradually increase once the habit becomes automatic.
